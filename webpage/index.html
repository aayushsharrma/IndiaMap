02. Kerala Forest Tour!!

Kerala, located in the southwestern part of India, is home to a rich variety of forest ecosystems due to its unique geographical location and varied topography. The state's forests are generally categorized into Tropical Wet Evergreen Forests, Tropical Moist Deciduous Forests, Tropical Dry Deciduous Forests, Mountain Sub Tropical Forests, and Plantation Forests.

03. Assam Forest Tour!!

Assam, located in the northeastern part of India, is known for its rich and diverse forest cover that comprises a wide range of ecosystems, from tropical rainforests and deciduous forests to grasslands and wetlands. The state lies in the Indo-Burma Biodiversity Hotspot, one of the 36 global biodiversity hotspots identified by Conservation International, indicating its global importance for biodiversity conservation.

04. Uttrakhand Forest Tour!!

Uttarakhand, a state in the northern part of India, is known for its diverse and rich forests. Nestled in the lap of the Himalayas, the state's forests range from subtropical pine forests in the lower elevations to alpine meadows at higher altitudes. The state's forest cover is broadly classified into five categories: tropical, subtropical, sub-alpine, alpine, and temperate.

05. Himachal Forest Tour!!

Himachal Pradesh, located in the western Himalayas of India, is known for its verdant and diverse forests, which cover about 66.5% of the state's total geographical area. The forests of Himachal Pradesh vary greatly due to differences in altitude, ranging from the subtropical forests at lower elevations to alpine pastures at higher elevations.
